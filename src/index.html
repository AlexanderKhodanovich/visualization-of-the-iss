International Space Station™

The largest modular space station; An astronaut's home base; The pinnacle of human collaboration;
The International Space Station is a multinational project that serves as a microgravity and space research laboratory, where scientific research is conducted in astrobiology, astronomy, meteorology, physics, and more. The first module of the ISS, the Zarya Control Module, launched on November 20th, 1998, kickstarting the life of the ISS 250 miles above Earth's orbit. It currently weighs 460 tons, is approximately the size of a football field, and is planned to serve as a home base for future missions to the Moon, Mars, and other celestial objects.
